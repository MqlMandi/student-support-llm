=== COURSE REGISTRATION ===
All students register through the UDSM Academic Registration Information System (ARIS).
Payments for fees must be made using control numbers obtained through ARIS, payable at the nearest bank or via mobile money.

Registration deadlines:
- New undergraduate and postgraduate students: registration opens 17 November 2025 and closes 28 November 2025.
- Continuing students (Semester 1): registration opens 24 November 2025 and closes 5 December 2025.
- Continuing students (Semester 2): registration opens 6 April 2026 and closes 17 April 2026.

Course load: Full-time students must register for a minimum of 60 course credits and a maximum of 72 course credits per semester. Part-time students must register for a minimum of 48 and maximum of 96 credits per academic year.

Important rules:
- No student may start a course more than 3 weeks after the beginning of the semester.
- No student may withdraw from a course more than 4 weeks after the beginning of the semester (applications to the Head/Dean/Director required before this deadline).
- Subject/programme transfers are allowed only within the first 4 weeks of the first semester and only if the student meets the admission requirements for the new programme.
- Successful applicants are registered only after paying the required university fees.
- Fees paid are not refunded.

Contact: Directorate of Undergraduate Studies (DUS), dus@udsm.ac.tz, Tel: +255 222 410 544 / 410 545 / 410 513.

=== EXAMINATION RULES AND ACADEMIC CONDUCT ===
Eligibility: A student must complete 100% of all coursework assessment and learning activities (assignments, tests, quizzes, presentations, laboratory work, fieldwork) and must secure a minimum cumulative score of 40% of continuous assessment marks to be eligible to sit for the university examination. Students who do not meet these requirements will be barred from examinations.

Examination format: Coursework assessment normally constitutes 40% and the final university examination constitutes 60% of the total course assessment.

Absence from exams: A student who deliberately absents themselves without compelling reasons is deemed to have absconded and will be discontinued from the University.

Prohibited items in the examination room (unauthorized materials): mobile phones, smart watches, electronic devices, written or printed notes, soft drinks (except water where permitted), alcoholic drinks, or any other specified material.

Academic dishonesty includes:
- Plagiarism
- Possession of unauthorized materials during an examination
- Cheating or attempting to cheat (copying, giving assistance)
- Communicating with others inside or outside the examination room
- Impersonation (sitting for exam on behalf of another person)
- Destroying or falsifying evidence of misconduct
- Causing disturbance in or near the examination room
- Tampering with, altering, or fabricating examination records (including digital systems)
- Forging or using fraudulent academic documents

Penalties:
- Possession of unauthorized materials, plagiarism, or communicating during exams: discontinuation from the University.
- Cheating, copying, or impersonation: failure in the whole year's examination and discontinuation.
- Causing disturbance: failure and discontinuation.
- Tampering with records (digital): discontinuation, revocation of degree, permanent ban, and legal prosecution.
- Where irregularities are discovered after graduation, the University may revoke the award.

Reporting: All cases of academic dishonesty must be reported to the Senate Undergraduate Studies Committee through the Department/Institute/School/College using Form UDSM-EI-01.

Appeals: A candidate found guilty and aggrieved may appeal to the Senate in accordance with regulations 18 and 19 of the General University Examination Regulations (GUER).

=== GRADING SYSTEM AND GPA ===
UDSM uses the following grading scale:
A   = 70 – 100%
B+  = 60 – 69%
B   = 50 – 59%
C   = 40 – 49%  (minimum passing grade)
D   = 35 – 39%
E   = 0  – 34%

The minimum passing grade is C (40%). Any grade below C is a fail.

Coursework Assessment (CA) constitutes 40% of the total mark. The final university examination constitutes 60%. Some practical/research-based courses may have CA up to 100% with Senate approval.

Progress to next year: Full-time students must pass a minimum of 120 course credits and achieve a minimum overall GPA of 2.0 to proceed to the following year of study.

Supplementary eligibility: A student may be allowed to re-sit failed courses if they attained an overall GPA of 1.8 or above after the first sitting.

Carrying over courses: A student who achieves a GPA of 2.0 or above after supplementary examinations may carry over failed courses into the next year. The maximum grade for a carried-over or supplementary exam course is C.

=== SUPPLEMENTARY EXAMINATIONS ===
Students who fail courses in the first sitting may be eligible for supplementary (re-sit) examinations if their overall GPA after the first sitting is 1.8 or above.

The maximum grade obtainable in a supplementary examination is C (the minimum passing grade). This also applies to carried-over courses.

Supplementary examinations are held at least one month after the ordinary second-semester examinations.

=== ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS ===
Minimum Entrance Requirements (Direct Entry):
A candidate must have:
1. Certificate of Secondary Education (CSEE) with passes in FIVE approved subjects, THREE of which must be at Credit level, obtained before sitting for ACSEE.
2. AND at ACSEE: Two Principal Passes in appropriate subjects with total points from three subjects not below 5 (Arts programmes) or 4 (Science-based programmes).

Grade-to-point conversion: A=5, B=4, C=3, D=2, E=1, S=0.5, F=0. The minimum Principal Pass is grade E.
(For 2014 and 2015 graduates, use: A=5, B+=4, B=3, C=2, D=1, E=0.5, F=0; minimum Principal Pass is grade D.)

Note: Principal passes in Divinity or Islamic Knowledge are not counted.

Equivalent Entry (Diploma holders): An appropriate Diploma with GPA not less than 3.5 for Ordinary Diplomas, or B+ average for Teacher Education and Health diplomas, or Distinction for unclassified awards, or B for Full Technician Certificates, from a college fully registered by NACTE and approved by UDSM Senate.

Applicants from 8-4-4 education system countries (e.g., Kenya) must complete at least one year of university in their own country before applying to UDSM.

How to apply: All applications must be submitted online through https://udsm.Admission.ac.tz. Submitting false information or forged certificates is an offence and may lead to legal action and de-registration.

Contact: Director of Undergraduate Studies, dus@udsm.ac.tz, Tel: +255 222 410 544 / 410 545.

=== NATIONAL HEALTH INSURANCE FUND (NHIF) ===
It is mandatory for every undergraduate student at UDSM to have National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF) coverage. Students pay a specified amount through their ARIS3 account. Upon fulfilling this requirement, students receive an NHIF membership card allowing access to healthcare at the UDSM Hospital and all other accredited health facilities countrywide.

The UDSM Hospital and its dispensaries are accredited to provide services to NHIF beneficiaries.

NHIF activities at UDSM are coordinated by the Health and Catering Unit in collaboration with the NHIF Office.

Medical Capitation (NHIF contribution) as part of Direct University Costs: TZS 50,400 per year (Tanzanians) or TZS 168,000 per year (international students).

=== POSTPONEMENT AND DEFERRAL OF STUDIES ===
Students may not postpone studies after the effective commencement of an academic year except under special circumstances. Permission is granted upon producing satisfactory evidence and written approval from the sponsor.

Special circumstances that may qualify:
- Sickness (must submit an authentic medical report from a recognized hospital)
- Serious social problems (each case is considered on its own merit)
- Severe sponsorship problem

Maximum absence allowed: Students may be away for a maximum of 2 years and still be re-admitted into the same year of studies where they left off.

Students discontinued due to examination irregularities may re-apply after 3 years away, and must compete with other applicants for re-admission into first year.

Students who postpone examinations are assigned a "PEX" (Postponed Exam) grade. Students who have not completed coursework for acceptable reasons receive an "I" (Incomplete) grade. Both must be cleared during the special examinations for that academic year.

A student may postpone a maximum of 3 examinations per academic year, except with DVC-Academic approval for special circumstances.

Contact: Directorate of Students Services or your College/School Dean for postponement applications.

=== INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS ===
UDSM welcomes students from across the world. The Directorate of Internationalisation, Convocation and Advancement (DICA) manages international student affairs under the UDSM Internationalisation Policy (2022).

Fees: International students pay significantly higher tuition fees (approximately 5 to 14 times the Tanzanian fee). Refer to the TUITION FEES section for details.

Accommodation: Improved and expanded accommodation facilities are available for international students.

Student visa: International students must obtain the appropriate student visa/residence permit. Contact the DICA office or your relevant college/school for guidance.

An international student is defined as any student studying at UDSM (online or face-to-face) who is not a Tanzanian citizen and does not have permanent residency.

Applicants from 8-4-4 education systems (e.g., Kenya, Uganda) must complete at least one year of university in their home country before applying to UDSM undergraduate programmes.

Contact: Directorate of Internationalisation, Convocation and Advancement (DICA), University of Dar es Salaam.

=== SPIRITUAL AFFAIRS AND WORSHIP ===
UDSM promotes religious tolerance and inter-faith dialogue. All students and staff have complete freedom of worship based on their spiritual beliefs, within the limitations of the laws of Tanzania and the Student By-Laws.

Facilities available on the Mwalimu Julius K. Nyerere Mlimani Campus:
- Mosque: for Muslim students and staff.
- Joint Christian Chapel: for Christian worship services.
- Spiritual counselling services: provided by spiritual leaders and counsellors coordinated by DSTS.

The Directorate of Students Services (DSTS) is responsible for managing spiritual affairs at UDSM. The University facilitates coordination of spiritual activities to ensure they do not disrupt academic activities.

Students are encouraged to engage with their respective faith communities and can seek spiritual counselling through DSTS.

=== CAREER GUIDANCE AND ENTREPRENEURSHIP ===
Career guidance services are provided by the Directorate of Students Services (DSTS), academic departments, and the UDSM Counselling Unit.

Services available:
- Career counselling sessions and mentorship programmes.
- Career days and job fairs with prospective employers.
- Soft skills training: communication, teamwork, collaboration, time management, adaptability, and critical thinking.
- Partnerships with industry for internships, field placements, and practical experience.
- Alumni engagement and employer connections for graduate job placement.
- Entrepreneurship training through the Directorate of Innovation and Entrepreneurship (DIEN).

Entrepreneurship: DIEN offers innovation, creativity, and entrepreneurship training. The Directorate aims to produce graduates who are job creators, not just job seekers. Students are encouraged to participate in DIEN activities.

Part-time employment: Postgraduate students may take part-time jobs as per UDSM General Regulations (2018). Undergraduate students are also encouraged to engage in entrepreneurship, peer tutoring, technical support, and other activities.

Contact: DSTS or the Counselling Unit for career guidance appointments. DIEN email: udiec@udsm.ac.tz, Tel: +255 22 2410115 Ext. 2087.
